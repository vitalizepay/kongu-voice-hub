Salem District

🌾 Steel City • Mango Capital • Gateway to Yercaud Hills
Tamil Nadu, India

Overview

Salem district is a major urban and industrial hub in Tamil Nadu, known for its steel plants, textile industries, sago production, and mango orchards. Nestled near the scenic Shevaroy Hills, the district is home to the popular hill station Yercaud. Salem city serves as the district headquarters and is one of the largest municipalities in the state.

Historically part of the ancient Kongu Nadu region, Salem flourished under the Cholas, Nayaks, and later the British. Today, it's a vibrant blend of tradition and modern industry.

Quick Facts

  • Area: 5,245 km²
  • Population: ~3.7 million (2024 est.)
  • Languages: Tamil, English
  • Major Industries: Steel, Textiles, Sago, Mango processing
  • Famous Dams: Mettur Dam, Stanley Reservoir
  • Hill Station: Yercaud (Shevaroy Hills)

Top Attractions

  • Yercaud Lake & Pagoda Point
  • Kottai Mariamman Temple (Salem Fort)
  • Mettur Dam & Hogenakkal Falls (nearby)
  • Kurumbapatti Zoological Park
  • Sugavaneshwarar Temple (1000+ years old)
  • Mango & Sago markets – Famous for 'Salem Mango'

Culture & Economy

Salem is often called the "Mango City" because of its large-scale mango cultivation and trade. It's also the largest producer of sago and starch in India. The district hosts a major industrial complex, including Salem Steel Plant (SAIL). Traditional arts like Karagattam, Oyilattam, and folk music are part of local festivals. The annual Kottai Mariamman Temple festival draws thousands of devotees.
