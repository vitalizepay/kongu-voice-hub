Karur, situated on the banks of the Amaravathi and Kaveri rivers, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited towns in Tamil Nadu. Historically a thriving trading hub linked to the ancient Cheras, the district today is a powerhouse of export-oriented home textiles — bed linens, kitchen linens, and wall hangings — as well as a renowned center for bus building and cement manufacturing (Chettinad Cement, TNPL)[reference:0][reference:1]. The region is bounded by Namakkal, Dindigul, Tiruchirapalli, and Erode districts, and the city of Karur serves as the administrative headquarters and a fast-growing municipal corporation[reference:2].

With a vibrant cultural heritage, the district is home to ancient temples such as the Pasupatheeswarar Siva temple and Thiruvithuvakkodu Vishnu temple, alongside historic mosques and a strong agricultural economy. Karur has evolved into a major industrial city while preserving its deep-rooted traditions.

⚡ Key industry: Over 70% of India’s home textile exports from the Kongu region pass through Karur’s value chain.

Karur contributes ~40% of India’s kitchen linen exports. Over 500 export units generate massive employment.
📖 Heritage
Pasupatheeswarar Temple (7th century), medieval Chera inscriptions, and Amaravathi dam make it a cultural treasure.

Karur is known as the “bus building capital” — manufactures bodies for state transport corporations across India.
